Understanding Pronouns and Verbs

What is a Pronoun?
A pronoun is a word we use instead of a name or noun. We use it to avoid repeating names all the time.
In English: I, you, he, she, it, we, they.
In Tounsi: Pronoun ya3ni kelma nasta3mlouha bch ma n3awdouch esm. Mathalan: "Sara is smart. Sara studies a lot." → "Sara is smart. She studies a lot." → "Sara fey9a. Hiya t9ra barcha."

🔹 What is a Verb?
A verb is an action word. It shows what the subject (person/thing) is doing.
In English: run, eat, talk, sleep, read...
In Tounsi: Verb ya3ni fi3l, chnowa l7aja li ta9a3. Mathalan: "He runs fast." → "Houwa yerkodh b sur3a."

Examples:

  • I speak English. → Ana netkallem anglais.
  • You play well. → Enti tel3eb behi.
  • She loves music. → Hiya t7eb el mousi9a.
  • It is cold. → Il meteo bared.
  • We are studying. → A7na na9raou.
  • They travel a lot. → Houma ysefrou barcha.

Exercise 1: Fill in the correct pronoun

  1. _____ is my best friend. (Ali)
  2. My sister is a doctor. _____ helps many people.
  3. This is my cat. _____ loves milk.
  4. _____ live near the beach. (My parents and I)

Answers and Explanation:

  1. He is my best friend. → Ali = He
  2. She helps many people. → Sister = She
  3. It loves milk. → Cat = It
  4. We live near the beach. → My parents and I = We

Exercise 2: Choose the correct verb form

  1. He _____ (like / likes) chocolate.
  2. They _____ (walk / walks) to school.
  3. I _____ (read / reads) every night.
  4. It _____ (make / makes) noise.

Answers and Explanation:

  1. likes → He = singular → verb with -s.
  2. walk → They = plural → verb without -s.
  3. read → I always takes base form (no -s).
  4. makes → It = singular → verb with -s.

💡 Tip: In the Present Simple, we usually add "-s" to the verb with "he / she / it".

Understanding Adjectives

🔹 What is an Adjective?
An adjective describes a noun. It tells us more about a person, place, or thing.
In English: big, small, beautiful, cold, smart...
In Tounsi: Adjective ya3ni sifat. Tfasir chnowa na3mlou bih. Mathalan: "The car is fast." → "Esaïyara sari3a."

Examples:

  • The house is big. → Eddar kbirra.
  • He is funny. → Houwa ydhak.
  • My sister is kind. → O5ti 7nouna.
  • The book is interesting. → El ktab mthell.

Understanding Adverbs

🔹 What is an Adverb?
An adverb describes a verb. It tells us how, when, where, or how often something happens.
In English: slowly, quickly, always, here, very...
In Tounsi: Adverb ya3ni kelma tfassir fi3l. Mathalan: "He walks slowly." → "Houwa yemchi bchwaya."

Examples:

  • She sings beautifully. → Hiya tghanni b jamel.
  • They always arrive early. → Houma dayman yjiw b krib.
  • I run quickly. → Ana nerkodh b sur3a.
  • He speaks loudly. → Houwa y7ki b sout 3ali.

💡 Difference between Adjectives and Adverbs

  • Adjective describes a noun. → "The girl is smart."
  • Adverb describes a verb. → "She studies hard."

In Tounsi: Adjective yfasar esm (sifa): "Weld moujtahed."
Adverb yfasar fi3l (kifach): "Houwa yqra b ijtihad."

Understanding Prepositions

🔹 What is a Preposition?
A preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and another word in the sentence. It often tells us about place, time, or direction.
In English: in, on, at, under, behind, before, after, to, from...
In Tounsi: Preposition (7arf jar) ya3ni kelma to9oulna win wela waqt chay. Mathalan: \"The book is on the table.\" → \"El ktab 3la ettawla.\"

Examples:

  • The phone is on the bed. → Ettéléphone 3la esserir.
  • The keys are in the bag. → Mfatih fi es-skak.
  • He came from school. → Ja men el madrassa.
  • I will go to the market. → Bch nemchi l sou9.
  • The cat is under the chair. → El qattous ta7t el kursi.

💡 Difference between Prepositions and Adverbs/Adjectives

  • Prepositions tell us the position/time/direction of something (with noun). → \"The book is under the table.\"
  • Adverbs describe how something happens. → \"He runs quickly.\"
  • Adjectives describe a noun. → \"It is a big table.\"

In Tounsi:
Preposition: ta3ti ma7el (blasa, waqt, jihha): \"el ktab fo9 ettawla.\"
Adverb: kifach sar fi3l: \"yodrob b 9owa.\"
Adjective: sifat lel esm: \"ktab kbir.\"\n

Understanding Conjunctions

🔹 What is a Conjunction?
A conjunction is a word that connects words, phrases, or sentences. It helps ideas stick together and flow smoothly.
In English: and, but, or, so, because, although, if...
In Tounsi: Conjunction (7arf 3atf) ya3ni kelma tawa77ed kalimat wala jomal. Mathalan: \"I like tea and coffee.\" → \"N7eb atay w 9ahwa.\"

Examples:

  • I like apples and bananas. → N7eb touffah w banane.
  • She is tired, but she is working. → Hiya ta3bana ama 9a3da tekhdem.
  • Do you want pizza or pasta? → T7eb pizza wala ma9rouna?
  • He stayed home because it was raining. → B9a fel dar 5ater kan shteya.
  • If you study, you will succeed. → Ila te9ra, bch tenja7.

💡 Comparison: Conjunctions vs Others

  • Conjunctions connect things: \"I am tired but happy.\"
  • Prepositions show place/time: \"The cat is under the bed.\"
  • Adverbs describe how: \"He runs fast.\"
  • Adjectives describe a noun: \"She is beautiful.\"

Conjunction: yربط kelmat/jomal: \"ya3ml w y9ra.\"

Understanding Interjections

🔹 What is an Interjection?
An interjection is a short word or phrase used to express strong emotion or reaction. It is often followed by an exclamation mark (!).
In English: wow, oh, ouch, hey, huh, ah, yikes...
In Tounsi: Interjection ya3ni kalma wala s7a w9sira nasta3mlouha bch n3abrou 3la cha3our (fer7a, ta3jb, waja3, 5oufa, etc). Mathalan: \"Wow! This is amazing!\" → \"Waw! Hatha rahi haja ray3a!\"

Examples:

  • Wow! That’s a beautiful dress. → Waw! Fessta jeyya chay7a.
  • Ouch! That hurts! → Aï! Tawa wje3tni!
  • Oh no! I forgot my keys. → Yaa 5sara! Nesit el mfatih.
  • Hey! Watch out! → Ey! Rou7 men houni!
  • Yay! We won the game! → Yeeey! Rbe7na el matche!

💡 Comparison: Interjections vs Others

  • Interjection = strong emotion → “Wow! This is amazing!”
  • Conjunction = connects words/sentences → “She is tired but happy.”
  • Adverb = describes how/when → “He walks slowly.”
  • Adjective = describes a noun → “It’s a cold night.”

In Tounsi:
Interjection = ta3bir 3la 7assa wala cha3our: \"Waw!\" \"Yaa!\"
Conjunction = yربط kelmat: \"ama, wela\"
Adverb = kifach wala imta: \"bser3a, alyoum\"
Adjective = sifat: \"kbira, zeyna\"\n
